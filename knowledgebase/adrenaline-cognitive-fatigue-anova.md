## Experience Scope
Designed and analyzed a factorial experiment studying the effects of adrenaline and cognitive fatigue on athletic performance. 96 participants assigned across 8 conditions, with blocking by age and gender to reduce variance. Focused on experimental design, statistical testing, model assumptions, ANOVA, post hoc testing, and diagnostic visualization.

## Research Problem
Investigated whether adrenaline and cognitive fatigue influence athletic performance, and whether interaction effects exist between conditions. Statistical goal: test main effects and interactions using a three-way ANOVA framework.

## Responsibilities
- Designing a 2×2×2 factorial experiment.
- Structuring 8 treatment conditions.
- Blocking participants by age and gender.
- Collecting or preparing participant-level data.
- Conducting three-way ANOVA in R.
- Running Tukey post hoc tests.
- Checking assumptions of normality and homoscedasticity.
- Applying log transformation.
- Creating diagnostic plots in ggplot2.
- Interpreting statistical significance at α = 0.05.

## Technical Contributions
Experimental design; factorial treatment structure; blocking strategy; ANOVA modeling; post hoc comparison; model diagnostics; log transformation; visualization of assumptions; statistical interpretation.

## Goals and Impact
- Designed a 96-participant factorial experiment.
- Tested 8 experimental conditions.
- Found no significant interactions at α = 0.05.
- Verified assumptions using log transformation and ggplot2 diagnostics.
- Confirmed normality and homoscedasticity after transformation.
